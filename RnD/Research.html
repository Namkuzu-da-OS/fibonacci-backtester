Core Philosophy

DiNapoli's Approach

DiNapoli's methodology strips Fibonacci analysis down to its most practical and profitable elements. Unlike many who use every possible Fibonacci ratio, DiNapoli focuses exclusively on what works in real trading.

"I use what works! I'm not a Fibonacci zealot."

- Joe DiNapoli

What DiNapoli USES

  • .382 and .618 ratios for retracements
  • .618, 1.0, 1.618 ratios for expansions
  • Price axis analysis only
  • Extremes (highs and lows)
  • Confluence and Agreement concepts

What DiNapoli Does NOT Use

  • Time axis Fibonacci
  • Fibonacci numbers (only ratios)
  • Arcs, ovals, spirals, bands
  • Minor ratios (.09, .146, .236, .50, .786, etc.)
  • Closes or averages for calculations

Critical Rule

"Fibonacci analysis should only be applied in the proper CONTEXT, with proper training, and as a part of an overall plan."

Key Definitions

Understanding these definitions is essential to mastering DiNapoli Levels. Refer back to them as needed.

Market Swing
A trader-defined market move, lasting minutes or years, taken from a "significant" market low or high to the most recent high or low. Also called a "wave."
Focus Number (F)
The extreme of the Market Swing. This is the location from which ALL retracement values (Fibnodes) for a given swing are calculated. If the Focus Number changes, all Fibnodes change.
Reaction Number (Point)
A high or low point within a given Market Swing. The Primary Reaction (*) is the extreme point - the lowest low in an up move or highest high in a down move.
Fibnode (Node)
A number based on Fibonacci retracement ratios (.382 or .618) that provides support when approached from above, or resistance when approached from below.
Objective Point (OP)
A number based on Fibonacci expansion ratios that marks a targeted profit objective. Three types: COP (.618), OP (1.0), and XOP (1.618).
Confluence (K)
When two Fibnodes from different Reaction Numbers have the same or nearly same value. Must occur between a .382 and .618 Fibnode. Creates significantly stronger S/R than a single Fibnode.
Agreement
When the proximity of a Fibnode and an Objective Point (COP, OP, or XOP) is "acceptably close." Creates high-probability zones.
Logical Profit Objective (LPO)
A predetermined price point where you place orders in the opposite direction. Can come from Objective Points, Fibnodes, or Confluence areas.

Fibnodes (Retracements)

The Two Fibnode Levels

DiNapoli uses only two retracement ratios. Two Fibnodes are calculated per Market Swing - one at .382 and one at .618 between the Focus Number and each Reaction Number.

Fibnode Equations

F3 = B - 0.382(B - A)
F3 = The 3/8 Fibnode (.382 retracement)

F5 = B - 0.618(B - A)
F5 = The 5/8 Fibnode (.618 retracement) - The Golden Ratio

Where: A = Start of move, B = Focus Number (the extreme/end of move)

"Retracement theory states that you measure the vertical distance of the wave between these two extremes of price (points A and B)... I said extremes of this wave, not closes, not hourly medians... It's the high and low you are interested in!"

- Joe DiNapoli, Chapter 8

When Approached From Above

Fibnodes provide SUPPORT

  • Look to buy above the Fibnode
  • Place stop below the Fibnode
  • F5 (.618) is stronger than F3 (.382)

When Approached From Below

Fibnodes provide RESISTANCE

  • Look to sell below the Fibnode
  • Place stop above the Fibnode
  • F5 (.618) is stronger than F3 (.382)

Key Insight: Lineage Markings

Always know which Reaction Number created each Fibnode pair. "A bunch of lines splayed across a chart with no identifying Lineage only confuses our action." Use semi-circular arcs to visually connect Fibnodes to their source Reaction Numbers.

Objective Points (Expansions)

The 3-Point ABC System

Objective Points are profit targets calculated using three points:

  • Point A = Start of the initial move
  • Point B = End of the initial move (the thrust)
  • Point C = End of the retracement

CRITICAL: Calculate From Point C, NOT Point B!

"Some previous works on this subject... suggest that the expansion start at the B high or low, rather than at the Point C retracement. My research and experience lead me to disagree with this. Use Point C to begin your expansion."

Objective Point Equations

COP = 0.618(B - A) + C
Contracted Objective Point (smallest target)

OP = (B - A) + C
Objective Point (1.0 = 100%) - Most commonly hit first

XOP = 1.618(B - A) + C
Expanded Objective Point (largest target)

COP (0.618)

Contracted Objective Point

The smallest/nearest target. Conservative profit objective.

OP (1.0)

Objective Point

The most common target - hit more often than COP before significant retracement.

XOP (1.618)

Expanded Objective Point

The largest target. Least frequently fulfilled. Strong directional moves only.

Which Target to Use?

"The strength of the market during the AB leg, as well as the lack of strength or depth of the retracements on the BC leg, help us to determine which of the three price objective targets is initially met."

  • Shallow retracement + strong thrust = aim for OP or XOP
  • Deep retracement + weak thrust = take COP
  • Consider scaling out at multiple levels

Confluence

When Fibnodes Align

Confluence occurs when two Fibnodes from different Reaction Numbers have the same or nearly the same numerical value. This creates a support/resistance zone that is significantly stronger than any single Fibnode.

Rules for Confluence

  • Must be between a .382 and a .618 Fibnode
  • Fibnodes must come from different Reaction Numbers
  • "Closeness" depends on volatility and time frame
  • Subjective - keeps non-judgmental traders confused

Why Confluence Matters

  • Much stronger S/R than single Fibnodes
  • Higher probability reversal zones
  • Better stop placement areas
  • Can act as Directional Signal on weekly/monthly

"Finally, if I can get a Confluence area off of a thrust bar that is not an identifiable reaction low, I am an excited trader. I have strong information the world lacks. I'm looking to make some serious money!"

- Joe DiNapoli, Chapter 11
6

Agreement

When Fibnodes and Objective Points Cluster

Agreement occurs when the proximity of a Fibnode and an Objective Point (COP, OP, or XOP) is "acceptably close." This creates extremely high-probability trading zones.

Trading with Agreement

DiNapoli's preference: "Enter just above the first .382 support Node" when there is Agreement between that Node and an OP expansion. Agreement provides confidence to be more aggressive with entries.

Trading Tactics

DiNapoli describes several entry and stop placement techniques. Choose based on your psychological makeup, experience, and market conditions.

BONSAI Aggressive

Entry: Enter at a pre-calculated D-Level (X)

Stop: Preset money/point stop (Y) - not based on D-Levels

Rules:

  • If stopped out and price returns above X, re-enter at market
  • If stopped out and price stays below X, check context, find deeper D-Level
  • Typical stops: 55-85 points (5-min S&P), 3-5/32nds (5-min Bonds)

Best for: Former floor traders, those who prefer simplicity over accuracy, high-frequency traders

BUSHES Moderate

Entry: Buy above one D-Level

Stop: Hide stop behind another (deeper) D-Level

The Analogy: "Hide behind the bushes, jump up, take a shot, and duck back down behind the bushes."

  • Stop has "cover" from the D-Level support
  • Better fills on stops due to support manifesting
  • This is DiNapoli's most common approach

Best for: Most traders - balanced risk/reward

MINESWEEPER A Conservative

Concept: Wait for support to manifest BEFORE calculating entry

  1. Have pre-calculated D-Level entry at point X
  2. Wait for support to manifest at Point 1 (near X)
  3. Wait for a move up to a new Focus (F)
  4. Calculate NEW Fibnodes from that up move
  5. Enter above the new .382 Fibnode
  6. Stop below .618 Fibnode or original low at Point 1

Best for: Those who want confirmation before entry, avoids getting stopped out on deep penetrations

MINESWEEPER B Most Conservative

Same as Minesweeper A, but wait for a Confluence area to form before entering.

  • Entry above Confluence area
  • Stop below Confluence area
  • "The bush is larger and thicker"
  • Drop time frame to achieve more reaction lows for Confluence

Best for: Daily-based players using hourly charts, risk-averse traders

Tactic Entry Based On Stop Based On Win Rate Risk
Bonsai D-Level Money stop Lower Higher
Bushes D-Level Deeper D-Level Medium Medium
Minesweeper A New Fibnode after bounce Deeper Fibnode Higher Lower
Minesweeper B New Confluence after bounce Below Confluence Highest Lowest

Time Frame Analysis

Less Is More

"Individuals often challenge themselves up to the limit of their ability and beyond. Some traders believe that keeping track of 23 indicators and 94 support levels provides them with more comprehensive analysis than two indicators and six support levels. In trading for profit, look at more markets if you must, but don't foul yourself up with a spaghetti chart."

Increasing Time Frame

(5-min hourly daily weekly monthly)

  • Fewer Reaction Points visible
  • Fewer DiNapoli Levels
  • Fewer Confluence areas
  • Bigger picture context

Decreasing Time Frame

(monthly weekly daily hourly 5-min)

  • More Reaction Points visible
  • More DiNapoli Levels
  • More Confluence areas
  • Fine-tune entries and stops

Practical Application

"If you are a daily-based player, but your equipment gives you the ability to collect hourly data... You can produce more Fibnodes on an hourly basis than you would otherwise see on the daily chart. You can therefore fine tune entry areas and stop placement points."

Always Check Higher Time Frames

"You should always study the higher Time Frame charts, so you know where you are in the bigger picture." Monthly and weekly D-Levels will affect your 5-minute trades!

Common Mistakes

DiNapoli's Prohibitions

  • Using closes or averages instead of extremes (highs/lows)
  • Starting expansions from Point B instead of Point C
  • Using too many Fibonacci ratios - stick to .382 and .618 for retracements
  • Ignoring context - Fibonacci levels need confirmation from trend analysis
  • Creating Confluence from same-type Fibnodes - must be .382 with .618
  • Not knowing Lineage - unidentifiable lines across a chart only confuse
  • Not checking higher time frames - missing the bigger picture
  • Thinking the Primary Node is something it's not - always go back far enough in history

Pruning the Fib Series

As Fibnodes are penetrated by price action, remove them from consideration. A Fibnode that has been taken out is no longer active. Keep your charts clean.

Exception: A brief penetration that immediately recovers may still be valid. Drop to a lower time frame to see how sharp the penetration was.

The Three Traders

DiNapoli uses three fictional characters throughout the book to illustrate different trading personalities and their outcomes:

Hyper Hank

The emotional, impulsive trader who acts before thinking.

  • Doesn't wait for retracements
  • Gets terrible fills
  • Forgets stop placement
  • Argues with brokers
  • Burns through capital
  • Goes through bite plates

Outcome: Perpetually frustrated, breaks even at best

Diligent Dan

The disciplined, level-headed trader who follows the rules.

  • Waits for retracements
  • Pre-calculates D-Levels
  • Places proper stops
  • Takes logical profits
  • Understands floor mechanics
  • Takes vacations with profits

Outcome: Consistent profitability

Conservative Carl

The overly cautious trader who often misses opportunities.

  • Waits too long to enter
  • Wants "extra confirmation"
  • Uses very wide stops
  • Misses moves waiting for perfection
  • Sometimes right approach for context

Outcome: Preserves capital but limited gains

"I've contrasted the actions of a level-headed trader and an emotional trader to show you how personality traits can impact performance."

- Joe DiNapoli, Chapter 12

Pre-Trade Checklist

  • Context confirmed (Trend direction, higher time frame analysis)
  • D-Levels calculated from EXTREMES (not closes)
  • Lineage identified for all Fibnodes
  • Confluence areas identified (if any)
  • Objective Points calculated from Point C
  • Agreement zones identified (if any)
  • Entry tactic selected (Bonsai, Bushes, Minesweeper)
  • Stop placement determined
  • Logical Profit Objective selected
  • Higher time frame D-Levels checked
